NON - GOVERNMENTAL ORGANIZATIONS

What is NGO?

It is an independent, non-profit group of citizens organized on a local, national, or international level to address social, environmental, or humanitarian issues. Operating without direct government control, they primarily focus on public welfare rather than generating profit.

Social Services Provided by NGOs

Helps Poor and Needy People

NGOs provide food, clothes, shelter, and basic support to underprivileged people.

Supporting and rescuing animals

Dedicated to animal welfare, feeding 50+ stray animals daily through our volunteer network.

Promotes Education

They help children and adults get access to education, books, scholarships, and skill training

Improves Healthcare

NGOs organize health camps, blood donation drives, medical awareness programs, and free treatments.

Supports Women Empowerment

They encourage women through education, self-employment training, and awareness programs.

Protects Environment and enhances Greenery

NGOs conduct tree plantation drives, cleanliness campaigns, and environmental awareness activities.

Creates Social Awareness

They spread awareness about issues like child labor, sanitation, gender equality, and human rights.

Provides Disaster Relief

During floods, earthquakes, pandemics, and other disasters, NGOs provide emergency help and rehabilitation.

Encourages Volunteering

NGOs give people opportunities to serve society and participate in community development.

Supports Rural Development

They work for village development by improving education, healthcare, water supply, and employment.

Acts as a Bridge Between Society and Government

NGOs help communicate public problems to the government and support welfare programs.

Promotes Equality and Human Rights

They work against discrimination and support equal opportunities for all people.

Develops Communities

NGOs help communities become stronger, independent, and socially responsible.

Provides Employment and Skill Development

Many NGOs offer vocational training and employment opportunities for youth and women.

Helps in Nation Building

Through social service and community welfare, NGOs contribute to the development of the country.

Encourages Social Responsibility

NGOs inspire people to care for society and participate in positive social change

  1. InAmigos Foundation

    • InAmigos Foundation was founded on September 23, 2020, by Mr. Govind Shukla (Founder & CEO).
    • Section 8 registered non-profit organization, licensed by the Central Government
    • It has its base at Chhattisgarh.
    • It holds 80G & 12A certifications, ensuring transparency, accountability and tax-exempt benefits for donors.
    • CSR-1 registered, allowing us to collaborate with corporate partners for impactful Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) initiatives
    • NITI Aayog registered, aligning our work with national development goals and hold the prestigious IAF ISO 9001:2015 certification
    • InAmigos Key Initiatives
      1. Project Seva : Providing food and clothing to the underprivileged
      2. Project Bachpanshala : Ensuring quality education for underprivileged children
      3. Project Jeev : Animal welfare, including rescue, protection and feeding
      4. Project Udaan : Women empowerment through skill development and financial independence
      5. Project Prakriti : Environmental conservation and sustainability efforts
      6. Project Vikas : Enhancing employability through skill development programs

  2. Akshaya Patra Foundation

    • OUR VISION: No child in India shall be deprived of education because of hunger.
    • OUR MISSION: Serve mid-day meals to 3 million children every day and achieve 3 million servings of morning nutrition
    • Feeding Programmes
      1. PM POSHAN (Mid-Day Meal Scheme) : The school lunch programme mainly covers children studying in primary (I-V) and upper primary (VI-VIII) classes in government and government-aided schools. In some states, children in secondary (IX-X) classes are also included
      2. ICDS Anganwadi Feeding Programme : The target beneficiaries of ICDS feeding programme comprise pregnant women, nursing mothers and children in the age group of 0-6 years.
      3. Breakfast Programme : It is designed and implemented to help address short-term hunger among school children and improve their concentration levels.
      4. Food Assistance : While most of the efforts over the years have been at the regional level, during the COVID-19 pandemic, food relief was extended across the country.
      5. Feeding Homeless Mothers : In Vrindavan, Uttar Pradesh, cooked meals are provided to homeless women who are vulnerable to hunger.
    • Non-feeding Programmes
    • National Endeavour for Student Transformation (NEST)
      1. Digital Education : An attempt to bridge the digital divide and further digital inclusiveness by providing students belonging to economically weaker sections of society access to good quality, modern education free of cost.
      2. Scholarship Programme : The initiative identifies deserving government school and college students from the underserved sections of society and helps them source scholarships so that they can pursue further education.
      3. School Rejuvenation Programme : Based on the Government’s Model School initiative, this endeavour aims to provide a conducive environment for children through a series of infrastructural and academic interventions.
      4. Abhay : A remedial classes initiative aimed at boosting children’s academic performance through activity-based learning and extracurricular activities.
